# [1st Place] HackUSU 2026: Marketing Analytics

**Project:** Optimizing a Protein Product for Red Button Vintage Creamery
**Tech Stack:** `Conjoint Analysis` | `Market Simulation` | `DAGitty` | `Data Analytics`

---

## The Problem

Red Button Vintage Creamery has a successful, traditional ice cream line but faced the challenge of **strategic expansion** into the health-conscious market. The core issue was determining which new product, at what price and feature combination, would most effectively attract college students without cannibalizing existing sales.

## The Solution: Data-Driven Conjoint Analysis

Our team conducted a comprehensive **Market Research Study** within a 24-hour window, surveying **188 college students** to generate **2,632 unique data points**.

### Methodology

* **Causal Modeling:** We utilized **DAGitty** to specify relevant attributes including calories, protein content, lifestyle, and dietary restrictions.
* **Conjoint Survey:** We designed a choice-based survey with 5 levels of product types and flavors to measure precise consumer utility.
* **Findings:** Data revealed that **Price** (23.58% importance) and **Product Type** (19.03%) were the primary drivers, but **Protein Content** emerged as a critical trend, holding more utility than sugar or calorie amounts[cite: 153, 233, 234].

## The Result: 1st Place Recommendation

We recommended greenlighting a **Cookies & Cream Protein Ice Cream**.

### Predicted Impact

* **Market Share:** Based on our simulator, **37% of the market** would pay a **$4.03 premium** for protein-fortified ice cream over standard options.
* **Profitability:** We predicted an increase of **$3.63 in carton-level profit** and a decrease in operating expense ratio from 35% to 25%.
* **Scalability:** This strategy results in a projected **$363K profit increase per 100K units sold**.
