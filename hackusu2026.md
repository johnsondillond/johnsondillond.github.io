# [1st Place] HackUSU 2026: Marketing Analytics

[cite_start]**Project:** Optimizing a Protein Product for Red Button Vintage Creamery [cite: 5]  
**Tech Stack:** `Conjoint Analysis` | `Market Simulation` | `DAGitty` | [cite_start]`Data Analytics` [cite: 82, 87]

---

## The Problem

[cite_start]Red Button Vintage Creamery has a successful, traditional ice cream line but faced the challenge of **strategic expansion** into the health-conscious market[cite: 38, 54]. [cite_start]The core issue was determining which new product, at what price and feature combination, would most effectively attract college students without cannibalizing existing sales[cite: 53].

## The Solution: Data-Driven Conjoint Analysis

[cite_start]Our team conducted a comprehensive **Market Research Study** within a 24-hour window, surveying **188 college students** to generate **2,632 unique data points**[cite: 117].

### Methodology

* [cite_start]**Causal Modeling:** We utilized **DAGitty** to specify relevant attributes including calories, protein content, lifestyle, and dietary restrictions[cite: 87].
* [cite_start]**Conjoint Survey:** We designed a choice-based survey with 5 levels of product types and flavors to measure precise consumer utility[cite: 106].
* [cite_start]**Findings:** Data revealed that **Price** (23.58% importance) and **Product Type** (19.03%) were the primary drivers, but **Protein Content** emerged as a critical trend, holding more utility than sugar or calorie amounts[cite: 153, 233, 234].

## The Result: 1st Place Recommendation

[cite_start]We recommended greenlighting a **Cookies & Cream Protein Ice Cream**[cite: 71, 255]. 

### Predicted Impact

* [cite_start]**Market Share:** Based on our simulator, **37% of the market** would pay a **$4.03 premium** for protein-fortified ice cream over standard options[cite: 296].
* [cite_start]**Profitability:** We predicted an increase of **$3.63 in carton-level profit** and a decrease in operating expense ratio from 35% to 25%[cite: 72].
* [cite_start]**Scalability:** This strategy results in a projected **$363K profit increase per 100K units sold**[cite: 73].
